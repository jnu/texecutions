Death Row in Texas

On June 26, Texas executed its 500th inmate since reinstating the death penalty in 1982. Of these 500, there have been at least two cases of probable wrongful execution: Cameron Todd Willingham and Carlos DeLuna.

Who are they?

Everyone on death row has been convicted of murder. Of these, just one, the 500th, was female. Nearly half of those executed were white, just over a third were black, and the remaining sixth were hispanic. The majority, about 60%, were under the age of 30 when they were incarcerated. Nearly 3/4 of inmates did not complete high school, with half dropping out before the 11th grade.

Last Words

Texas has published the last statements made by their inmates before execution. Some inmates recite Bible verses or poetry, others apologize to the victim's family, and still others give a last-moment prayer to the Cowboys. A few inmates take this time to protest their conviction, but many use it to express thoughts of love and thankfulness. God and Allah are also frequently referenced. The more disconcerting of them are from prisoners who do not seem fully aware of what's happening, that they would have an opportunity to speak, or that there would be an audience, for example prisoner 999095. By far the most uncomfortable to read are those whose final words continue through the moment a lethal cocktail of chemicals is injected into their veins and they comment on its sting, and even taste.
